üö® Online Scams

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent emails or messages that appear to be from trusted sources, designed to steal your personal information, passwords, or financial details.

Example: An email claiming to be from your bank asking you to "verify your account" by clicking a link.

Online Shopping Scams

Fake e-commerce websites or social media ads offering products at unbelievably low prices. After payment, you never receive the product.

Red Flags: Too-good-to-be-true prices, no customer reviews, poor website design, no return policy.

Investment & Lottery Scams

Promises of high returns with little risk, or notifications that you've won a lottery you never entered. These require upfront payment or personal details.

Remember: If you didn't buy a ticket, you didn't win. Legitimate investments always carry disclosed risks.

Romance Scams

Scammers create fake profiles on dating apps or social media, build emotional relationships, then ask for money for emergencies.

Warning Sign: They profess love quickly, avoid meeting in person, and eventually ask for financial help.

üì± Fake Calls & Messages

Impersonation Calls

Fraudsters pose as bank officials, government authorities, or tech support claiming there's an issue with your account or device.

Common Tactics: Creating urgency, threatening legal action, or offering refunds to extract information.

OTP Scams

Callers claim to be from your bank and ask you to share the OTP (One-Time Password) sent to your phone "for verification purposes."

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Rule: NEVER share your OTP with anyone. Banks never ask for OTPs over calls.

WhatsApp & SMS Scams

Messages from unknown numbers claiming to be family members in distress, lottery wins, or links to "urgent videos" that install malware.

Action: Verify independently before responding. Don't click suspicious links.

KYC Update Scams

Messages or calls stating your KYC (Know Your Customer) details need immediate updating, directing you to fake websites to steal your information.

Fact: Banks notify KYC updates through official apps and never ask for complete details via links.

üîê Cyber Safety Tips

  • Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Create passwords with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password across multiple sites. Consider using a password manager.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security to your accounts. Even if someone gets your password, they can't access your account without the second factor.
  • Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your operating system, apps, and antivirus software. Updates often include security patches for vulnerabilities.
  • Be Cautious with Public Wi-Fi: Avoid accessing banking or sensitive information on public networks. If necessary, use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) for encryption.
  • Verify Before You Click: Hover over links to see the actual URL before clicking. Be suspicious of shortened URLs or links from unknown sources.
  • Think Before You Share: Be mindful of what you post on social media. Oversharing personal details can make you vulnerable to identity theft and targeted scams.
  • Secure Your Devices: Use screen locks, biometric authentication, and enable remote wipe features in case your device is lost or stolen.
  • Regular Backups: Back up important data regularly to external drives or cloud storage to protect against ransomware and data loss.

üîí Protecting Personal Data

What is Personal Data?

Any information that can identify you: name, address, phone number, email, Aadhaar number, PAN, bank details, photos, IP address, and more.

Limit Data Sharing

Share personal information only when absolutely necessary. Question why an app or service needs specific permissions or data.

Example: Does a flashlight app really need access to your contacts and location?

Review Privacy Settings

Regularly check privacy settings on social media, apps, and online accounts. Limit who can see your posts and personal information.

Be Careful with App Permissions

When installing apps, review the permissions they request. Deny unnecessary permissions like camera, microphone, or location access if not essential to the app's function.

Secure Your Documents

Never share photos of your Aadhaar, PAN, passport, or other identity documents on public platforms or with unverified sources. Use password-protected PDFs when sharing digitally.

Monitor Your Accounts

Regularly check bank statements and credit reports for suspicious activity. Set up alerts for transactions above certain amounts.

Dispose Data Securely

Before selling or discarding devices, perform a factory reset and ensure all personal data is completely erased. Delete old accounts you no longer use.
